Bear Lion Shark
Bears are large, solitary mammals of the Ursidae family,
found across North America, South America, Europe, and Asia. 		Lions (Panthera leo) are large, social carnivorous cats, known as
"king of the beasts," living primarily in Sub-Saharan African
savannas and a small, endangered population in India's Gir Forest. 		Sharks are cartilaginous fish, not mammals, characterized by skeletons
made of flexible cartilage rather than bone.
  • Male black bears typically weigh 125-500 lbs, while grizzlies
    range from 400 to over 1,000 lbs
  • They measure 5-6 feet from tip of nose to the tip of their tail
  • Bears are opportunistic omnivores
  • Bears in the wild generally live between 15 and 30 years
  • Adult male lions typically weigh between 330-570 pounds
  • Adult male lions typically measure 1.7 to 2.5 meters
  • Lions are strict, obligate carnivores that primarily hunt large mammals
  • Lions typically live for 10-14 years in the wild, though some can reach
    16-18 years.
  • Shark weights vary dramatically by species, ranging from under 1 lb
    for small species to over 40,000 lbs
  • Adult male lions typically measure 1.7 to 2.5 meters
  • Lions are strict, obligate carnivores that primarily hunt large mammals
  • Most shark species live between 20 and 30 years in the wild
